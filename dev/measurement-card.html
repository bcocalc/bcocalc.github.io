    Line Stop Math

    Quick formulas and field notes for Standard Line Stop, HTP, and Hi-Stop. This is the fast field-reference version, not the full training deck.

    Standard Line Stop Set Point = MD +/- LD + POD - Wall Use for conventional line stop heads when you need lower-in from the training set-point method.
    HTP Stop TCO = MD +/- LD + PTC + BCO HTP BCO follows the pipe size chart. Plug Set uses PSD +/- LD.
    Hi-Stop Cutout TCO = (RL/2) + (POD/2) + CL + PTC Verify reamer length, cutter length, and pilot-to-cutter before trusting the cutout mark.
    Hi-Stop Plug Set Plug Set = PSD + MD +/- LD + 1/2 POD PSD = ((RCD - PB) / (2 x Plug Taper)) + 0.25.
    Hi-Stop Notes Minimum wall thickness is schedule 40 Training notes also call out 12 o'clock as the preferred location and no PTC on 2" and smaller Hi-Stops.
    Completion Plug Pipeline LI = JBF + LD Pipeline side-outlet completion plug training also uses LI = FTD - TMU +/- LD.

        Field Checklists

        Short, mobile-first reminders for the parts that usually matter before a job is saved, shared, or handed to the next tech.

        1

        Job Setup

        • Customer, location, date, technician, and job number are filled in.
        • Machine and job type match the operation being run.
        • Notes capture anything the next person cannot see from the calculator numbers.
        2

        BCO / Geometry

        • Pipe material, nominal size, schedule, pipe ID, and cutter OD are verified.
        • BCO warning is clear before using the number in ETA or workflow.
        • Geometry lock is only used after the pipe and cutter values are confirmed.
        3

        Hot Tap

        • MD, LD sign, PTC, and machine travel are checked against the physical setup.
        • ETA is using the current BCO and correct cutter size.
        • Coupon/cutter notes are captured before saving the job.
        4

        Line Stop

        • Variant is correct: Standard, HTP, or Hi-Stop.
        • Line Stop Travel is optional, but Machine Travel should be entered when you want the travel check.
        • Warnings are reviewed before marking the stage ready.
        5

        Completion Plug

        • Start, JBF, and plug travel fields are entered from the correct side of the job.
        • Completion plug stage is only needed when the loaded job has plug data or the operation requires it.
        • Save after plug numbers are entered so the shared job keeps the final operation state.
        6

        Before Save / Share

        • Operation dropdown shows every hot tap, line stop, or completion plug in the job.
        • Switch between operations once and confirm each card still shows its own values.
        • Save local first, then sync shared when the current job looks right.
